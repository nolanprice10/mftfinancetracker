Saving on Transportation

Lower your commute costs as a student

Transportation is a common student expense that can grow quickly. Whether you walk, bike, take public transit, or drive, there are ways to reduce costs. Small changes can save you significant money over a school year.

Use student discounts on transit

Many transit systems offer student passes or discounted fares. Check your local bus or train service. A discounted monthly pass often costs less than buying single tickets.

Walk or bike when possible

Walking and biking are free and healthy. If your school is close enough, these options can replace short rideshares or bus trips. Even walking a few days a week reduces transportation costs.

Carpool with friends

If you drive, sharing rides can reduce gas and parking costs. Set up a carpool schedule with classmates or friends who have similar routes. This saves money and can make commuting more social.

Plan errands efficiently

Combine errands to reduce unnecessary trips. For example, buy groceries and supplies in one trip instead of multiple. This saves time and transportation costs.

Track your transportation spending

Use a tracker to log transportation expenses. This helps you see patterns and decide where to cut back. Many students are surprised by how much they spend on occasional rideshares.

Why it matters

Transportation costs may seem small, but they add up. Saving just a few dollars a week can free money for savings or school expenses. With a little planning, you can keep transportation affordable and predictable.
